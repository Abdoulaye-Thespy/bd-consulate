Looking Back at the Bangladesh Economic Forum in Cameroon

  • Honorary Consulate

One year ago, the Bangladesh Economic Forum in Cameroon was successfully held in the grand hall of the Cameroon Chamber of Commerce, Industry, Mines and Handicrafts (CCIMA) in Douala. This landmark event marked a significant milestone in the economic relations between Bangladesh and the Central African region.

The forum brought together key stakeholders from both nations, creating a dynamic platform for dialogue and collaboration. Business leaders, policymakers, and entrepreneurs gathered to explore mutual opportunities and strengthen economic ties between Bangladesh and the CEMAC region.

"This forum represents a bridge between our economies, opening doors to new partnerships and shared prosperity between Bangladesh and Central Africa."

A Platform for Economic Exchange

This landmark event provided a valuable platform for exchange between the business community of Cameroon, the CEMAC region, and economic operators from Bangladesh. It showcased Bangladesh's vast potential in key sectors such as:

  • Agro-industry: Bangladesh's expertise in agricultural processing and food production
  • Garment industry: Showcasing Bangladesh's world-renowned textile and apparel manufacturing capabilities
  • Pharmaceuticals: Highlighting Bangladesh's growing pharmaceutical sector and quality medicines
  • Fisheries: Presenting opportunities in aquaculture and seafood processing
  • Other promising fields: Including IT, renewable energy, and leather goods

Sector-Specific Breakout Sessions

The forum featured specialized sessions focusing on different industries, allowing for in-depth discussions about collaboration opportunities. These sessions enabled participants to:

• Identify complementary strengths between Bangladeshi and Cameroonian businesses
• Discuss regulatory frameworks and trade facilitation measures
• Explore joint venture possibilities and investment opportunities
• Address challenges and solutions for cross-border trade

Strengthening Bilateral Relations

The Honorary Consulate of Bangladesh in Cameroon is proud to have contributed to this initiative and reaffirms its commitment to strengthening economic and trade relations between Bangladesh and the CEMAC sub-region. The success of this forum has paved the way for:

Follow-up business delegations between the two countries, with Cameroonian entrepreneurs visiting Bangladesh to explore manufacturing partnerships, and Bangladeshi business leaders traveling to Cameroon to assess market opportunities firsthand.

Enhanced diplomatic engagement at both governmental and private sector levels, with ongoing discussions about preferential trade agreements and investment protection frameworks that would benefit businesses from both nations.

Key Outcomes of the Forum

  • 15+ Memoranda of Understanding signed between Bangladeshi and Cameroonian companies
  • Identification of 5 priority sectors for immediate collaboration
  • Commitment to establish a Joint Business Council
  • Plans for reciprocal trade missions in the coming year

Looking Forward

As we reflect on the success of this forum one year later, we are encouraged by the progress made in Bangladesh-Cameroon economic relations. The connections forged during this event have already resulted in several business partnerships and increased trade volumes between our nations.

The Honorary Consulate remains dedicated to building on this momentum, facilitating more exchanges, and supporting the business communities of both countries as they explore the vast potential of our economic partnership.

We extend our gratitude to all participants, partners, and supporters who contributed to the success of the Bangladesh Economic Forum in Cameroon and look forward to continuing this important work in the years to come.
